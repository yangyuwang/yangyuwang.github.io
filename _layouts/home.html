My research examines the relationship between material culture and collective cognitive systems, asking how their interplay drives cultural change across multiple levels—from organizational identity to the coevolution of art and attitude. Drawing on the materiality and cognitive turns in cultural sociology, I develop multimodal computational methods to bridge embodied cultural objects (images, texts, sounds) with internalized schemas and belief structures. Through deep generative models and network analysis applied to historical and contemporary data, my work addresses how infrastructures shape meaning, how social learning mechanisms diffuse norms, and how coordinated changes in symbolic environments reshape visible identities and legitimate cultural categories.

A. Cultural Innovation and Evolution

These projects examine how material cultural artifacts—poems, paintings, and artistic classifications—evolve through social processes. Using computational methods including network analysis, computer vision, and generative AI, I trace innovation mechanisms across historical periods and social contexts. From Tang Dynasty poetry networks to gender representation in six centuries of paintings, to agent-based simulations of artist interactions, these studies reveal how network positions, institutional categories, and coordinated social action reshape symbolic forms and creative outputs, bridging the materiality turn in cultural sociology with quantitative cultural analysis.

B. Belief and Attitude Updates

These projects map how cognitive structures and internalized beliefs transform under rapid social change. Drawing on Chinese survey data spanning three decades, I analyze value network reconfigurations and life course transition patterns as individuals navigate economic development and rural-to-urban migration. Using network analysis and latent class modeling, these studies reveal both stability and diversification in belief systems, demonstrating how macro-level transformations interact with individual-level schemas to reshape normative expectations and behavioral pathways, contributing to the cognitive turn in cultural sociology.
